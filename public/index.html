What's the feeling?

This page is intended to help your normalize and manage your emotional responses. Through the use of IBM's "Watson", a "cognitive system" that "learns at scale", it is now possible to quickly and reliably draw up a series of indices corresponding to the emotional tenor of a given piece of writing.

It is difficult to say with any certainty how accurate Watson's calculations are. It pretends to a much greater degree of objectivity than any of us can, and so is accountable only to itself.

The fact of its being unreliable and possibly entirely off-base is no reason not to put it to good use, though.

Frequently I get emails. Sometimes these emails seem, to me, to have an emotional content that is difficult to contextualize. An oddly angry-sounding email from a boss. A melancholy email from someone buying my table through Craigslist. An inexplicably ebullient email from a distant family member.

Sometimes these emails leave me disoriented and confused. I need to reply, but am not sure I can pitch my own 'email voice' to match that of my correspondent. I struggle.

Thankfully, I do not have to struggle anymore, because I've built this website. Here's how it works:

  • In the first form, place the original email to which you are responding.
  • In the second form, place the response you have drafted.
  • Press the button and let Watson counsel you on how you sound to others. Revise accordingly.
